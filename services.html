Engagement models

Use Clarion and L-ide to build better tools, deliver clearer projects, and tackle problems that don’t fit standard boxes.

Talltree exists for people who want more than another dashboard or slide deck. We build and run workflows that start from first principles – making assumptions explicit, aligning stakeholders, and using AI where it actually helps. On this page you’ll find three ways to work with us: building tools, delivering projects, and clarifying the tough problems that don’t have a template.

Build

We design and build tools, internal interfaces, and AI integrations around your actual workflow – not around a vendor demo. Using Clarion and L-ide, we start by mapping your assumptions, constraints, and success criteria. Then we design and implement the smallest useful system that moves your team forward, with clear boundaries and room to grow.

  • Custom internal tools
  • AI-assisted workflows and agents
  • Data flows that match how your team actually works

Deliver

When you already have a project in motion – or a mandate from leadership – we help you actually land it. L-ide makes the assumptions, dependencies, and risks explicit. Clarion helps break the work into concrete, testable steps. You get a living project map: clear milestones, owners, and feedback loops, plus guidance on where AI can safely plug in.

  • Project framing and scope clarification
  • Assumption mapping and risk surfacing
  • AI integration planning and guardrails
  • Lightweight reporting that leaders will actually read

Clarify

Some problems don’t come labeled as “projects” yet. They show up as vague pain: a team that’s stuck, a process that never works, a strategic question you can’t quite frame. In Clarify sessions, you bring the messy reality; we bring our reasoning stack. Together we turn the situation into a clear problem statement, a map of assumptions, and a set of concrete next experiments or decisions.

  • Open-ended problem-solving and strategy sessions
  • “Weird problem” triage – technical, organizational, or both
  • Translating leadership vision into testable, executable work
