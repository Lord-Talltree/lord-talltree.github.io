L-ide

Turn assumptions into a shared map

L-ide captures the assumptions hiding inside your plans, so teams and AI systems can work from the same ground truth.

Unspoken assumptions are one of the fastest ways to derail a project. In human teams they show up as missed expectations, delays, and budget overruns. With AI systems they show up as prompt drift, rework, and wasted tokens. L-ide is a logic-driven module that takes your natural language – plans, briefs, prompts, specs – and turns it into a shared map of assumptions, dependencies, and open questions. That map gives project managers, C-suite leaders, and AI engineers a clear view of what the work is really built on, so they can adjust early instead of cleaning up late.

The Real Risk Isn’t The Plan – It’s The Assumptions Underneath It

Most plans look clean on paper, but they quietly depend on dozens of unspoken assumptions: about data quality, team capacity, stakeholder priorities, or even how an AI model will behave. Those assumptions stay invisible until something breaks – a deadline slips, a budget explodes, or a pilot fails. L-ide brings those assumptions to the surface before they hurt you.

Turn Natural Language Into A Logic Map

L-ide takes the way you already talk and write – project briefs, strategy docs, prompts, Slack threads – and translates it into a structured logic map.

That map highlights:

  • The assumptions you’re currently making
  • The constraints you’ve declared (or implied)
  • The dependencies between different parts of the plan
  • The gaps, conflicts, and open questions that need a decision

Built For People Who Need Clarity, Not Just Noise

L-ide is built for people who are accountable for outcomes, not just activity.

  • Project managers can keep work aligned with real constraints instead of wishful thinking.
  • C-suite and leadership can see the logic behind a plan before they commit budget and reputation.
  • AI engineers and data teams can turn high-level intent into clear, testable assumptions their systems can actually work with.

Fewer Surprises, Faster Decisions, Better Projects

With L-ide, you spend less time untangling misunderstandings and more time solving the real problem. Assumptions become visible, decisions become traceable, and teams – human or AI – can align on what “success” actually means before they start building.
